Tomato Soup

Description

Tomato soup is a classic comfort food that brings together the rich, tangy flavor of ripe tomatoes with a smooth, creamy texture. Often simmered with a blend of aromatic herbs like basil and thyme, this soup offers a vibrant red hue and a deliciously inviting aroma. The tomatoes, sometimes roasted to enhance their natural sweetness, are pureed to perfection, creating a velvety base that is both refreshing and hearty. A touch of cream or a swirl of olive oil adds a layer of richness, making each spoonful a balanced symphony of flavors.

Typically served hot, tomato soup can be enjoyed on its own or accompanied by a variety of side dishes such as a crispy grilled cheese sandwich or freshly baked bread. Its versatility allows for numerous variations, including the addition of ingredients like garlic, onions, carrots, and celery, which deepen the soup’s flavor profile. Garnishes like croutons, fresh basil leaves, or a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese can elevate the presentation and taste, transforming a simple dish into a gourmet experience. Whether savored on a chilly day or as a light starter, tomato soup remains a timeless favorite that warms both the body and soul.

Ingredients

  • Ripe tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Olive oil or butter
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Carrots (optional)
  • Celery (optional)
  • Tomato paste
  • Vegetable or chicken broth
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Dried thyme
  • Sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Heavy cream or milk (optional, for creaminess)
  • Croutons or bread (optional, for serving)
  • Parmesan cheese (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Chop the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery.
  2. Core and chop fresh tomatoes or open the canned tomatoes.
  3. Heat olive oil or butter in a large pot over medium heat.
  4. Sauté the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery until softened and onions are translucent.
  5. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes (or canned tomatoes) and vegetable or chicken broth.
  7. Season with fresh basil, dried thyme, salt, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  9. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth or blend in batches using a blender.
  10. Stir in heavy cream or milk if a creamier soup is desired.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  12. Serve hot, optionally garnished with croutons or Parmesan cheese.
