Cake

Description

A cake is a sweet, baked dessert commonly enjoyed during special occasions such as birthdays, weddings, and celebrations. It is typically made from a mixture of flour, sugar, eggs, butter or oil, and a leavening agent like baking powder or baking soda. Cakes come in various flavors, including vanilla, chocolate, red velvet, and lemon, and can incorporate fruits, spices, and other flavorings. They are often round or rectangular but can be shaped into nearly any form. Layers of cake are separated by rich fillings such as buttercream, ganache, or fruit preserves, and the outer surface is usually coated with icing or frosting, which can be smooth or intricately decorated.

Beyond traditional cakes, there are cheesecakes, mousse cakes, sponge cakes, and fruitcakes, each with unique ingredients and preparation methods. Some cakes are light and airy, like angel food cake, while others are dense and rich, like pound cake. Regardless of the type, cakes are a centerpiece of celebration, bringing joy and sweetness to any gathering.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Granulated sugar
  • Eggs
  • Unsalted butter
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Whole milk
  • Vanilla extract
  • Cocoa powder (optional, for chocolate cake)
  • Buttermilk (optional, for a richer texture)
  • Vegetable oil (optional, for added moisture)
  • Lemon zest or juice (optional, for lemon cake)
  • Food coloring (optional, for decorative purposes)
  • Spices (optional, such as cinnamon or nutmeg, for added flavor)
  • Powdered sugar (for frosting)
  • Butter or cream cheese (for frosting)
  • Heavy cream or milk (for frosting)
  • Flavor extracts (optional, such as almond or peppermint, for frosting)
  • Fruit preserves or jams (optional, for filling)

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Grease and flour the cake pans or line them with parchment paper.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together 2 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
  4. In a large bowl, beat 1 cup of unsalted butter (softened) until smooth.
  5. Gradually add 2 cups of granulated sugar to the butter and beat until light and fluffy.
  6. Add 4 large eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  7. Mix in 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract.
  8. Add the flour mixture to the butter mixture in three parts, alternating with 1 cup of whole milk, beginning and ending with the flour mixture.
  9. Mix until just combined.
  10. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans.
  11. Smooth the tops with a spatula.
  12. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  13. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn them out onto a wire rack to cool completely.
  14. In a large bowl, beat 1 cup of butter (softened) until creamy.
  15. Gradually add 4 cups of powdered sugar, 1/4 cup of heavy cream or milk, and 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract to the butter.
  16. Beat the frosting until smooth and fluffy.
  17. Once the cakes are completely cool, place one layer on a serving plate.
  18. Spread a layer of frosting or filling on top.
  19. Place the second layer on top and frost the top and sides of the cake.
  20. Decorate as desired and serve.
